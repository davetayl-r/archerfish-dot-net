We have built a detailed cost-benefit model based on an approach developed by the Washington State Institute for Public Policy. This involves building sectoral models of service provision to connect the often separate pillars that make up service delivery.

Our detailed Australian state-level cost-benefit model provides an accurate estimation of the potential monetised benefits and costs of evaluated policies and programs. Our modelling can be used by public sector agencies and non-government organisations at different levels of government to support funding applications for new, significant, or continuing policy initiatives.

Cost-benefit analysis (CBA) is a technique used to determine the benefits and costs associated with a particular policy intervention. Benefits and costs are expressed in monetary terms and are discounted by the time value of money, allowing costs and benefits that occur at different time periods to be expressed as net present value.

By monetising benefits, CBA allows decision makers to assess whether a policy intervention is a sound investment, and to compare it with competing policy options. In public policy, CBA has historically been limited to major capital projects — but it is increasingly being applied to health, education, social welfare, criminal justice, social services and housing, pioneered by the Washington State Institute for Public Policy.

What policy interventions work? A systematic review and meta-analysis of the literature identifies evidence-based options that have achieved measurable improvements in high-quality experimental evaluations.

What policy interventions make economic sense? Using panel surveys, administrative data and budget papers, the benefits and costs associated with each policy option are calculated.

What level of risk surrounds these estimates? Estimates and assumptions are subjected to a risk assessment using Monte Carlo simulation to calculate the return on investment for each intervention.

How to develop a portfolio of policy options? Portfolio analysis determines which combination of policy interventions provides the best return on investment for a given budget.
