Cost-benefit analysis (CBA) is a technique used to determine the benefits and costs associated with a particular policy intervention. Benefits and costs are expressed in monetary terms and are discounted by the time value of money, allowing costs and benefits that occur at different time periods to be expressed as net present value.

By monetising benefits, CBA allows decision makers to assess whether a policy intervention is a sound investment, and to compare it with competing policy options. In public policy, CBA has historically been limited to major capital projects — but it is increasingly being applied to health, education, social welfare, criminal justice, social services and housing, pioneered by the Washington State Institute for Public Policy.
